## Game Mechanics

### 1. The Trading System (Trade Wars Core)

**The Concept**:
Conference booths are "ports" that buy/sell different goods. Buy low at one booth, sell high at another. Market prices fluctuate based on supply/demand. Compete with other traders (NPCs) for profit.

**Goods Categories** (8 types):
1. **Business Cards** (common): Everyone wants these, low margin, high volume
2. **Swag** (common): T-shirts, stickers, pens, cheap but tradable
3. **Tech Demos** (uncommon): Valuable, booths pay well for these
4. **White Papers** (uncommon): Information goods, analysts love these
5. **Prototypes** (rare): Hardware samples, very valuable but hard to find
6. **Information** (rare): Insider knowledge, secrets, rumors (sell to fixers)
7. **Favors** (very rare): IOUs from important people, used for quests
8. **Contracts** (legendary): Job offers, partnerships, deals (end-game items)

**Booth Types** (Trade Wars "Ports"):

**Class 1 Booths** (Buying Booths):
- **Vendor Booths**: Buy swag, demos, prototypes (pay well)
- **Analyst Booths**: Buy white papers, information (high prices)
- **Recruiting Booths**: Buy business cards, favors (need employees)

**Class 2 Booths** (Selling Booths):
- **Marketing Booths**: Sell swag, business cards (cheap but abundant)
- **Demo Booths**: Sell tech demos (moderate price)
- **Innovation Booths**: Sell prototypes, white papers (expensive)

**Class 3 Booths** (Balanced):
- **Startup Booths**: Buy and sell everything (fair prices, balanced)
- **Megacorp Booths**: Buy and sell (manipulate prices, can be predatory)

**Trading Mechanics**:
- **Buy**: Pay booth's asking price (modified by Negotiation skill)
- **Sell**: Receive booth's offer (modified by Negotiation skill)
- **Haggle**: Negotiate +/- 10% (CHA check, costs 5 energy)
- **Bulk Deal**: Buy/sell 5+ of same item, get 15% discount/bonus
- **Corner Market**: Buy ALL of one item type from a booth (locks out competitors for 30 minutes)

**Price Dynamics** (Dynamic Economy):
- **Base Price**: Each good has a base value (Business Cards = 5cr, Prototypes = 250cr)
- **Supply Modifier**: More in stock = lower price, scarce = higher price
- **Demand Modifier**: High demand booths pay more, low demand pay less
- **Time Modifier**: Prices fluctuate every 10 minutes (simulate real-time market)
- **Competition**: Other traders affect prices (if NPC buys all swag, price spikes)

**Example Trade**:
```
You visit Booth #15 (TensorCorp - Vendor Booth, BUYING goods)

Inventory:
  - Business Cards: Buying at 8cr each (base 5cr + high demand)
  - Swag: Buying at 15cr each (base 10cr + medium demand)
  - Tech Demos: NOT BUYING (full stock)

Your Inventory:
  - Business Cards x10
  - Swag x5

Actions:
1. Sell Business Cards x10 (earn 80cr)
2. Sell Swag x5 (earn 75cr)
3. Haggle Business Card price to 9cr each (CHA check)
4. Sell all (earn 155cr total)
5. Ask about demand (Market Analysis check, learn what they'll buy later)
```

### 2. Reputation System (Upgrade Tiers)

**Reputation Milestones**:
- **Tier 0: Nobody** (0-20 rep)
  - Access: Main floor only
  - Prices: Default (no bonuses)
  - NPCs: Ignore you

- **Tier 1: Known Quantity** (21-40 rep)
  - Access: Main floor + vendor area
  - Prices: 5% better
  - NPCs: Politely acknowledge you
  - Unlock: Can enter vendor area competitions

- **Tier 2: Rising Star** (41-60 rep)
  - Access: Main floor + vendor area + VIP lounge
  - Prices: 10% better
  - NPCs: Seek you out, offer side quests
  - Unlock: Fixers appear, offer rare goods/quests

- **Tier 3: Industry Leader** (61-80 rep)
  - Access: All areas + speaker green room
  - Prices: 15% better
  - NPCs: Treat you as important, competitors wary
  - Unlock: Can give panels, affect market trends

- **Tier 4: Legend** (81-100 rep)
  - Access: Everything + secret back rooms
  - Prices: 20% better + can manipulate markets
  - NPCs: Beg for your time, job offers constant
  - Unlock: Can trigger special endings

**Gaining Reputation**:
- Complete trades (+1-3 rep per successful trade)
- Complete quests (+5-15 rep)
- Give presentations (+10 rep, requires Presentation 5+)
- Discover secrets (+8 rep)
- Corner markets (+5 rep, but -10 with affected NPCs)
- Attend Vogon poetry readings (+2 rep for surviving)

**Losing Reputation**:
- Scam NPCs (-10 rep)
- Break contracts (-20 rep)
- Insult important people (-5 rep)
- Get kicked out of panels (-8 rep)

### 3. NPCs with Memory (Persistent Personalities)

**NPC Categories**:

**Traders** (Competing Players):
- **The Whale**: Rich, buys everything, drives up prices (rival!)
- **The Shark**: Aggressive trader, corners markets, blocks you
- **The Minnow**: New trader, easy to outcompete, sometimes allies
- **The Flipper**: Day trader, buys low/sells high constantly (creates volatility)

**Fixers** (Quest Givers):
- **The Broker**: Trades in information, gives espionage quests
- **The Connector**: Introduces you to VIPs, networking quests
- **The Dealer**: Black market goods, morally gray quests
- **The Archivist**: Collects rare items, collection quests

**VIPs** (Major NPCs):
- **CEO of MegaCorp**: Can offer dream job (ending path)
- **Famous Engineer**: Technical guru, respects Tech Talk skill
- **Keynote Speaker**: Influencer, can boost your reputation
- **Investor**: Has money, looking for startups to fund

**Vendors** (Booth Staff):
- **Enthusiastic Intern**: Friendly, gives discounts if you're nice
- **Burned-Out Sales**: Hates job, will accept bribes to look away
- **True Believer**: Loves their product, gives quests to promote it
- **Corporate Drone**: Follows policy exactly, bureaucratic nightmare

**NPC Memory System**:
Each NPC tracks:
- **Relationship**: -100 to +100 (affected by your actions)
- **Conversations**: Remembers what you discussed
- **Trades**: Remembers if you screwed them over or treated fairly
- **Promises**: Tracks if you kept your word
- **Mood**: Happy/Neutral/Angry (affects prices and dialogue)

### 5. The Towel Mechanic (Hitchhiker's Influence)

**Your towel is the most important item in the game.**

**Towel Properties**:
- **Indestructible**: Cannot be lost, stolen, or destroyed
- **Multi-purpose**: Has 3 uses per day (5 if Towel Master trait)

**Towel Uses** (Creative Problem-Solving):

**Use 1: Distraction**
- Wave towel to distract security/NPCs
- Allows sneaking past guards, skipping queues
- **Example**: "You wave your towel frantically. The guard looks confused. You slip past into the VIP lounge."

**Use 2: Improvised Tool**
- Substitute for missing item
- Fix broken equipment
- **Example**: "You wrap the towel around the broken demo unit. It stabilizes! The vendor is impressed."

### 6. Bureaucracy Puzzles (Vogon Influence)

**The Concept**: Some quests and areas require navigating absurd bureaucratic procedures. These are logic puzzles wrapped in satirical humor.

**Puzzle Type 1: Form Hell**

```
You need to access the Secret Back Room. The guard hands you a stack of forms:

FORM 27B/6: "Application for Access to Restricted Area (Non-Authorized Personnel)"

Required fields:
  1. Full Name: ___________
  2. Employee ID: ___________ (but you're not an employee!)
  3. Reason for Access: ___________ (choose carefully!)
  4. Signature: ___________
  5. Manager Signature: ___________ (you don't have a manager!)
  6. Notary Stamp: ___________ (where's a notary at a tech conference?)
  7. Date: ___________ (hint: wrong date = rejected!)

Incorrect answers = form rejected, start over (costs 10 energy + 15 minutes)

Available options:
1. Fill form honestly (will probably fail, but ethical)
2. Lie on form (might work, requires Fast-Talk skill)
3. Bribe guard to skip form (costs 100cr)
4. Forge manager signature (requires Bullshit Detection 5+)
5. Find a notary (side quest: search conference for notary NPC)
6. Use towel to distract guard and sneak in
7. Give up (leave)
```

**Puzzle Type 2: Queue Management**

```
You need to talk to the CEO of MegaCorp. They're in Meeting Room 5, but there's a queue.

CURRENT QUEUE STATUS:
  Position 1: VIP (priority, cuts ahead)
  Position 2: Investor (priority, cuts ahead)
  Position 3: You (waiting...)
  Position 4: Engineer (also waiting)
  Position 5: Sales person (also waiting)

Wait time: 60 minutes (you don't have that much energy!)

Options:
1. Wait in queue (costs 60 energy, you only have 50!)
2. Use VIP badge to cut ahead (if you have one)
3. Bribe Position 2 to swap places (costs 200cr)
4. Intimidate Position 4 to let you ahead (Intimidation check)
5. Network with people in queue (might learn shortcut)
6. Find alternate route (Queue Management skill check)
7. Come back later (quest delayed)
```

**Puzzle Type 3: The Impossible Request**

```
Fixer: "I need you to get me a Prototype from Innovation Corp's booth."
You: "Sure, I'll buy one."
Fixer: "No, no. It has to be the RED prototype. They only have BLUE ones on display."
You: "So... where's the red one?"
Fixer: "In their storage room. Behind the booth."
You: "Can I just ask for it?"
Fixer: "They won't give it to you unless you have a Storage Access Voucher."
You: "Where do I get that?"
Fixer: "From the Logistics Office."
You: "Where's that?"
Fixer: "It's in the Meeting Rooms, but it's only open from 2-3pm."
You: (it's 4pm)
Fixer: "Better luck tomorrow!"

This is a Hitchhiker's-style puzzle: absurdly bureaucratic, but solvable with creativity.

Solutions:
1. Wait until tomorrow 2pm, go to Logistics Office (slow but works)
2. Bribe Innovation Corp staff to "misplace" red prototype (expensive)
3. Use Babel Fish skill to translate "Storage Access" into vendor-speak, confuse staff
4. Find alternate source of red prototypes (side quest)
5. Use towel to sneak into storage room
6. Convince fixer to accept blue prototype instead (Fast-Talk check)
```

### 7. Vogon Poetry Readings (Status Effect Survival)

**The Concept**: The worst panels imaginable. Attending inflicts status effects, but surviving grants reputation.

**Vogon Panel Types**:

**"Blockchain Synergies in Enterprise Cloud IoT"** (Tier 1: Mild):
- Status Effect: "Bored" (−2 to all actions for 10 minutes)
- Duration: 15 minutes
- Reward: +2 rep, 10cr
- Survival: Easy (INT check, DC 8)

**"Multi-Paradigm Object-Oriented Functional Reactive Programming"** (Tier 2: Severe):
- Status Effect: "Confused" (−5 to all actions, 50% chance to do random action)
- Duration: 30 minutes
- Reward: +5 rep, 30cr
- Survival: Hard (INT check, DC 12)

**"Vogon Poetry Reading: 'Ode to a Small Lump of Green Putty'"** (Tier 3: Extreme):
- Status Effect: "Existential Dread" (−10 to all actions, can't trade for 1 hour)
- Duration: 45 minutes
- Reward: +10 rep, 100cr, achievement "Survived Vogon Poetry"
- Survival: Very Hard (INT + LUCK check, DC 15, or use towel to protect yourself)

**Panel Survival**:
- Each turn, roll INT (or INT + LUCK for Vogon poetry)
- Success: Resist status effect, earn reward
- Failure: Gain status effect, might flee panel (lose reward)
- Critical Success: Enjoy the panel ironically, bonus +5 rep
- Critical Failure: Flee screaming, lose 5 rep

**Why Attend?**
- Reputation gains (important for tier upgrades)
- Some panels have VIP attendees (networking opportunities)
- Vogon poetry grants unique achievement
- Absurdist humor (it's funny to suffer through these)

## Style Guide

### Writing Principles

1. **Satirical but not cruel**: Poke fun at tech conferences, but with affection
2. **Absurdism with purpose**: Vogon poetry and bureaucracy teach patience and creativity
3. **Economic depth**: Trading should feel strategic, not random
4. **NPC persistence**: NPCs remember EVERYTHING, relationships matter
5. **Multiple valid paths**: No "correct" way to play, all endings are victories

### Tone Examples

**Trading**:
- "You sell 10 Business Cards to Booth #42. The vendor smiles: 'Ah, networking! The ancient art of exchanging tiny rectangles with strangers.' You earn 85cr."

**Vogon Poetry**:
- "The poetry continues. '*Grunthos the Flatulent / Whose writing was quite rancid...*' You feel your soul withering. Why did you attend this panel?"

**NPC Dialogue**:
- "Alice the Broker leans in: 'I've got a secret. The CEO of MegaCorp? They're not human. I mean metaphorically. They're a spreadsheet that gained sentience.'"

**Bureaucracy**:
- "The guard examines your Form 27B/6. 'This is Form 27B/6 revision 3. We're on revision 4 now. You'll need to start over.' You've lost 45 minutes of your life."

**Towel**:
- "You wave your towel at the security guard. They're mesmerized. 'That's... a very nice towel,' they say, stepping aside. You're in."

### Response Length
- **Trading**: 200-250 words (detailed market info, prices, options)
- **NPC interactions**: 250-300 words (dialogue, relationship tracking, memory callbacks)
- **Panels**: 150-200 words (fast-paced suffering)
- **Endings**: 300-400 words (dramatic, satisfying conclusion)
